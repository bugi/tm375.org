You are welcome to come as a visitor as often as you like.  If you find that you enjoy Toastmasters we hope that you will choose to join. An Application is attached.

 

ORGANIZATION of Meeting: 1 hour Meeting

 

6:15 - 6:30pm

Meeting participants and guests arrive, topic speakers and evaluators are chosen and given their assignments, and program leaders are organizing their portion of the meeting.

6:30- 6:35pm

Club business and Intro of Toastmaster assignments.

6:35 – 7:30pm

The educational portion of the meeting, lead by the Toastmaster, is carried out by different program leaders, see listings below.  We try to adjourn at 8:00 pm.

 

BUSINESS MEETING: The Business meeting is run by the assigned Chairperson. This person conducts business such as introducing guests, secretary and treasurer reports, committee reports, old and new business, and announcements. The chairperson also introduces the Toastmaster of the evening.

 

EDUCATIONAL  PORTION:  The following is a typical layout for the educational portion of the meeting.  Exceptions occur for variety, speech contests, and special educational sessions.  The program leaders are encouraged to use their creativity in carrying out their part of the program.

 

TOASTMASTER:

Runs the educational portion of the meeting and keeps the meeting moving. The Toastmaster is responsible for introducing each of the program leaders and the speakers. The TM contacts members prior to the meeting and prepares and agenda of program leaders and speakers.

 

HUMORIST: Can be the Toastmaster

We try to start the meeting with a joke or funny story. It gives an opportunity for people to gain some confidence with humor or story telling and it starts the meeting off in a light mood.

 

WORDSMITH:

A word is presented and defined, with examples how it can be used in a sentence.  The word is usually one that people often misuse or one that is rarely used.  The speakers and program leaders then try to use the word in their presentations. The Wordsmith may also be assigned as Grammarian.

 

TABLE TOPICS:

The Topic Master brings to the meeting three or four topics which he/she gives to club members who do not have a duty for the evening.  The topics are given out either before the meeting starts or at the time of the talk.  The member gives a 1-2 minute impromptu speech on the topic.  The topics can be on anything, for example, recent events in the news, favorite vacation, or goals.

 

SPEECHES:

Each evening one or two prepared speeches are presented.  These are often oriented toward improving one or more speaking skills as outlined in a manual.  The first manual contains important basics on speaking which will be practiced in the first ten speeches given.  For example, there might be an emphasis on vocal variety or use of gestures. 

 

Once completing this manual there are advanced manuals which guide development of further speaking skills.  Three advanced manuals are a part of the initial one time membership charge.
EVALUATIONS:

The General Evaluator organizes this portion of the meeting. He/She also chooses Evaluators, the Timer, and Grammarian, who is also the “ah” counter. The Grammarian is often assigned to the Wordsmith. Following the prepared speeches there is an Evaluator for each speech who gives his or her opinions about the speech.  The evaluation covers areas suggested in the Manual, and where the speaker has improved, outstanding parts of the speech and delivery, and suggestions for improvement.  We emphasize that this is only ONE person’s point of view.  Other members give their opinions by writing a note during a one minute of silence after each speech and passing it to the speaker or by talking with the speaker after the meeting.  

 

AWARDS:

To promote recognition for improvement and outstanding presentations, we give out awards each night for best topic speaker, prepared speaker, evaluator, and program leader.  The members and visitors vote and pass ballots to the leader of that portion of the meeting.  

 

TIMER:

One member uses a stopwatch and a set of lights to help the speaker or evaluator be aware of his/her time.  Most speeches are 5-7 minutes, topic speeches are 1-2 minutes, and evaluations are 2-3 minutes. The Timing Box has details relating to when the Green, Yellow, and Red lights are illuminated.

 

SCHEDULES:

We have a schedule, which designates duties for members several weeks in advance.  The abbreviations for participants are as follows: CH Chairperson, TM Toastmaster, TT Topicmaster, GE General Evaluator, H Humorist, WS Wordsmith, S Speaker, Ed Educator, E Evaluator and X-- if the person has a planned absence.  The Vice President of Education makes up this schedule, preferably using dates requested by members.  Occasionally members ask to be considered temporarily inactive when they will have to miss several consecutive meetings (dues are still paid).

 

MEMBERSHIP FEES:  $45 every 6 months, pro-rated by month ($7.50 International + $0 Club) based on April to October and October to April periods. If you are new to the Toastmasters organization there are new member fees of $20 International and $ 0 Club. The Secretary or Treasurer fills out the form for new membership. Or you can check on-line for further information. Currently we are not collecting Club Dues and Fees since our treasury is doing well. This can change. Most likely it would be $1 added per month. www.toastmasters.org.

 

Following is a list of guidelines presently used in our club:

·         We try to never leave the lectern unattended.  The person finishing a presentation or introduction waits for the next person to meet him/her at the lectern (and usually shake hands) before he/she sits down.

 

·         Follow program leader whether or not to applaud after an introduction.

 

·         Try not to say “Thank You” at the end of a speech. Audience thanks you with applause.

 

·         If a speaker needs to rearrange the table or set up a visual aid then he/she should do so prior to recognizing the toastmaster and before addressing the audience.  Time starts with the speaker’s first words.

 

·         It is the responsibility of each member to find an alternate if they will not be attending a meeting for which they have a scheduled duty.

 

·         The Toastmaster for the following week will Email or call all of the members that have a duty to verify their participation.

 

·         New members are encouraged to choose and request another member to be their mentor.
